Investment Options


Large-cap stocks refer to companies with a market capitalization over $10 billion. These companies are typically well-established, financially stable, and have a proven track record of growth.

Theyâ€™re often seen as lower-risk investments compared to smaller caps due to their ability to withstand market fluctuations, but their growth potential may be more modest.

Mid-cap stocks have a market capitalization between $2 billion and $10 billion. Positioned between large and small caps, they offer a balance of stability and growth potential.

Mid-cap companies are often in the growth phase, expanding their market presence and profitability, making them attractive for investors seeking moderate risk with potential for good returns.

Small-cap stocks have a market capitalization below $2 billion. These companies are generally newer or in the early stages of growth, which makes them more volatile but with higher potential for significant gains.

While they carry more risk than larger caps, small-cap stocks can appeal to investors looking for long-term growth opportunities.

Growth stocks are shares in companies that are expected to grow faster than the overall market. They reinvest earnings to expand, which can lead to high returns for investors willing to take on more risk.

These stocks are popular among investors looking for long-term capital appreciation rather than immediate income.

Dividend stocks belong to companies that pay out a portion of earnings to shareholders regularly. They provide a steady income stream, making them attractive for income-focused investors.

These stocks are often associated with stable, established companies with less volatility.

Value stocks are shares of companies that appear to be undervalued relative to their fundamentals, such as earnings and book value. Investors buy them hoping they will appreciate over time.

They appeal to investors looking for long-term growth with reduced downside risk.
